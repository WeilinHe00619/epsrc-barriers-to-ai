EPSRC Funded Research Project

Exploring Barriers to
AI Doctoral Studies

We are an interdisciplinary team from the University of Bristol dedicated to uncovering the socio-economic, literacy, and structural factors influencing students' pursuit of AI PhDs.

Explore Research Details
Partners: EPSRC | University of Bristol

Socio-economic Barriers

Investigating how gender, race, household background, and financial pressure form "invisible gates" that filter talent before the application stage.

AI Literacy & Confidence

Utilising the AILIT-S test to assess student literacy. Examining how self-efficacy acts as a psychological gatekeeper in career decisions.

Structural & Policy Barriers

Through NLP analysis of 2,643 PhD listings from FindAPhD, revealing inequalities in recruitment policies and funding distribution.

Mixed-Methods Design

"Getting Started in AI" Training

Co-designed with the Jean Golding Institute (JGI) to provide interventionist training for students with non-technical backgrounds.

Pre/Post Quantitative Tracking

Measuring quantitative changes in students' AI knowledge, attitudes, and further study intentions following the intervention.

Qualitative In-depth Interviews

Conducting 45-60 minute semi-structured interviews to uncover deep-seated views on career expectations and institutional policies.

# Research Workflow
1. Recruitment & Pre-training Survey
2. "Getting Started in AI" Training
3. Post-training Survey & Vouchers
4. Interviews & Thematic Analysis

Core Insights

01

Global Ambition vs. National Restriction

"While 79.2% of funded projects claim to be open globally, high visa costs and the 30% international recruitment cap create a 'False Hope' barrier."

02

The Structural Confidence Gap

"Even with identical academic backgrounds, students from lower socio-economic backgrounds show significantly lower self-efficacy in AI literacy self-assessments."
